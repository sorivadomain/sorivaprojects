Frequently Asked Questions

+

What is the mission of Manus Dei Secondary School?

Manus Dei aims to provide a holistic education rooted in Catholic values, fostering academic excellence, moral integrity, and spiritual growth to prepare students for leadership and service.

+

Is Manus Dei a boarding or day school?

Manus Dei is a co-educational Roman Catholic boarding school, offering a safe and nurturing residential environment for all students.

+

What grades does the school offer?

The school offers secondary education from Form 1 to Form 4, preparing students for the Certificate of Secondary Education Examination (CSEE).

+

What extracurricular activities are available?

Students can participate in football, netball, debate clubs, music, arts, and community service, promoting teamwork and leadership skills.

+

How does the school support spiritual growth?

As a Catholic institution, we offer daily prayers, Mass, catechism classes, and spiritual retreats to nurture students’ faith and values.

+

What are the admission requirements?

Admission requires passing an entrance exam, submitting academic records, and aligning with the school’s Catholic ethos. Contact us for details.

+

How is academic performance at Manus Dei?

The school consistently achieves high performance in CSEE, with many students earning Division I and II results in science and arts subjects.

+

Are there scholarships or financial aid options?

Limited scholarships are available based on merit and need. Interested families should contact the administration for application details.

+

What facilities does the school provide?

Manus Dei offers modern classrooms, science labs, a library, sports fields, and comfortable boarding facilities to support learning and growth.

+

How can parents stay involved with the school?

Parents are encouraged to attend meetings, participate in events, and communicate with teachers via scheduled visits or our contact channels.
